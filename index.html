How to play the games
🏃 How to play · The Chase main›

Dr. Vidya's Quiz Show is a TV-style race against the Quiz-Bot 3000 — outrun the bot across 1,062 NCLEX-style questions covering every week of the course.

  1. Choose a mode. From the start screen pick a single week, the Midterm Chase (Weeks 1–6), the Final Chase (Weeks 8–14), Adaptive Review (drills the topics you miss most), or the Daily Challenge (a fresh 20-question set each day).
  2. Set difficulty & length. Pick Easy, Standard, or Hard — this changes your head-start, lifelines, timer, and scoring — then a round length: Quick 10, Standard 20, or Marathon 30.
  3. Answer to run. Each correct answer sprints your contestant a step toward 🏁 safety; a wrong answer — or letting the timer run out — lets the Quiz-Bot surge a step closer. Stay ahead to win the round.
  4. Select-all-that-apply. Some questions need every correct option — choose them all, then press Lock in answer (it's all-or-nothing).
  5. Earn & use lifelines. Build streaks to earn 50:50 (removes two wrong choices), ❄️ Freeze (blocks one bot step), and ⏱️ Slow-Mo (adds time). Spend them when a question stumps you.
  6. Build streak combos. Consecutive correct answers escalate through tiers (×3 → ×15) for bonus points and confetti — keep the chain alive.
  7. Learn from every answer. After each question, read the full rationale and tap the 📖 slide reference to review that exact topic.
  8. Clear the boss. Each chapter has a themed boss; beat it to earn mastery stamps, then review the end-of-round analytics — accuracy by topic and exactly what to study next.
  9. Extras. Spend earned coins in the Shop, browse the Bestiary of bosses, chase achievements, and save & resume any round — close the tab and pick up later.
▶ Play The Chase
🗺️ How to play · PathoQuest›

A pixel-art adventure RPG where each body system is a zone guarded by a disease boss. Travel the world and battle your way to mastery.

  1. Move around. On a computer use the arrow keys (or WASD); on a tablet or phone use the on-screen D-pad. Walk your character across the world map.
  2. Enter a zone. Each zone is a body system — step onto it to begin its challenge.
  3. Battle by answering. Enemies and the zone boss attack with NCLEX-style questions. Answer correctly to damage them and earn XP; a wrong answer lets the enemy strike back and you lose health.
  4. Read the teaching tip shown after each question — absorbing those is the whole point.
  5. Defeat the boss to clear the zone, earn rewards and cards, and pass the mastery gate that unlocks the next area.
  6. Mind your health. If it runs low, answer carefully to survive and recover.
  7. Collect & progress. Grow your card collection, track XP and mastery, and work through every zone to complete the quest.
  8. Controls recap: move with arrows / WASD / D-pad; pick an answer by tapping or clicking it (or D-pad + confirm).
▶ Play PathoQuest
🩺 How to play · Medical Mystery›

You're the clinician. Work a real patient case inside an EHR: gather evidence, reach a diagnosis, then prove you understand the pathophysiology and the nursing priorities.

  1. Read the patient banner. Name, demographics, chief complaint, and vital signs sit at the top — just like a real chart.
  2. Work the EHR. Use the tabs to interview the patient, do a physical exam, order labs, and request imaging. Each action costs diagnostic points, so gather what you need without wasting moves.
  3. Build your case. Watch how each finding narrows the differential — order strategically instead of everything at once.
  4. Submit a diagnosis. When you have enough evidence, open the differential and choose the best fit. If you're wrong, the attending nudges you — you get up to 3 attempts.
  5. 🧬 Patho Mode. After diagnosing, explain the pathophysiology in your own words; you're scored on how many key concepts you include, so be thorough.
  6. 📋 NCLEX Mode. Then prioritize nursing interventions (what do you do FIRST?), choose the right medications, and answer an NCLEX-style question — scored on clinical judgment and priority.
  7. Get your grade. You're scored on Diagnosis, Efficiency (point use), and Pathophysiology. Nail all three — correct diagnosis + strong patho + an efficient workup — to earn Outstanding.
  8. Replay cases to sharpen your efficiency and reasoning.

Guide to the Tools

New here? Here's what each resource is and how to get the most out of it. Every tool covers the same material in a different way — mix and match to whatever helps it stick.

🎧

Podcasts on Spotify

Short audio episodes that talk through each week's big ideas like a conversation. Put one on in the car, on a walk, or at the gym — they're perfect for absorbing the material hands-free and for a quick refresher right before an exam.

📚

Interactive Books on H5P

More than reading. These digital books have questions, clickable images, and activities built right in, checking your understanding as you go and giving you instant feedback. Move at your own pace and redo any section until it clicks.

🎨

Illustrated Workbooks flipbooks

Page-turning, heavily illustrated workbooks that turn dense pathophysiology into clear pictures and step-by-step diagrams. Ideal if you learn best by seeing a process laid out rather than reading about it.

🎮

Kahoots quiz game

Fast, fun multiple-choice quiz games. Use one to gut-check whether you really know a topic, race the clock on your own, or play with classmates. A quick, low-stakes way to find your weak spots before they cost you on a test.

📝

Study Guides view or PDF

Your complete review for the week — every concept organized with mechanisms, "why it matters" notes, exam tips, mnemonics, and practice questions. Read it in your browser, or download the PDF to print and highlight.

📊

Slides download

The actual lecture slide decks, yours to keep. Follow along during class, review what was covered, or use them to fill the gaps in your own notes.

🃏

Flashcards in-hub

Seventy self-test cards for every week — flip, rate yourself Again or Got it, and each deck tracks what you’ve mastered. A date-seeded daily challenge quizzes you across all weeks and keeps a streak. The fastest way to drill terms and mechanisms before an exam.

🫀

Interactive Lessons in-hub

Eighty-six hands-on, animated pathophysiology lessons — click, drag, and toggle to watch each mechanism unfold, plus live ABG and Punnett-square calculators. The most interactive way to make a concept finally click.

📈 A simple way to study each week

  1. Meet the concepts with the Interactive Book or Slides.
  2. Hear them explained another way with the Podcast.
  3. See the visuals in the Illustrated Workbook.
  4. Tie it all together with the Study Guide.
  5. Drill the terms with the Flashcards and take the daily challenge.
  6. Test yourself with the Kahoot.
  7. Lock it in by playing The Chase and PathoQuest.
