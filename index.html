Guide to the Tools

New here? Here's what each resource is and how to get the most out of it. Every tool covers the same material in a different way — mix and match to whatever helps it stick.

🎧

Podcasts on Spotify

Short audio episodes that talk through each week's big ideas like a conversation. Put one on in the car, on a walk, or at the gym — they're perfect for absorbing the material hands-free and for a quick refresher right before an exam.

📚

Interactive Books on H5P

More than reading. These digital books have questions, clickable images, and activities built right in, checking your understanding as you go and giving you instant feedback. Move at your own pace and redo any section until it clicks.

🎨

Illustrated Workbooks flipbooks

Page-turning, heavily illustrated workbooks that turn dense pathophysiology into clear pictures and step-by-step diagrams. Ideal if you learn best by seeing a process laid out rather than reading about it.

🎮

Kahoots quiz game

Fast, fun multiple-choice quiz games. Use one to gut-check whether you really know a topic, race the clock on your own, or play with classmates. A quick, low-stakes way to find your weak spots before they cost you on a test.

📝

Study Guides view or PDF

Your complete review for the week — every concept organized with mechanisms, "why it matters" notes, exam tips, mnemonics, and practice questions. Read it in your browser, or download the PDF to print and highlight.

📊

Slides download

The actual lecture slide decks, yours to keep. Follow along during class, review what was covered, or use them to fill the gaps in your own notes.

The Chase game · main

A TV-style quiz-show race against the Quiz-Bot through 1,062 NCLEX-style questions covering every week. Play a single week, the Midterm Chase (Weeks 1–6), or the Final Chase (Weeks 8–14). Every miss lets the bot close in, so it turns recall into a heart-pounding race — a powerful (and genuinely addictive) way to drill for exams.

How to play
  1. Pick a week, the Midterm Chase, or the Final Chase — then choose round length (10/20/30) and difficulty.
  2. Answer before the timer runs out: correct answers sprint you toward safety; wrong answers or timeouts let the Quiz-Bot gain a step.
  3. Build answer streaks to earn lifelines — 50:50, ❄️ Freeze, and ⏱️ Slow-Mo.
  4. After each answer, read the full rationale and tap the slide reference to review that topic.
  5. Stay ahead of the bot to win, then check your end-of-round analytics (accuracy by topic + what to review) and unlock achievements.
🗺️

PathoQuest game

A pixel-art adventure RPG where each body system is a zone guarded by a themed disease "boss." Explore a top-down world and battle bosses using your pathophysiology knowledge, collecting cards and rewards as you go — serious review disguised as a quest.

How to play
  1. Move your character around the world map — D-pad on a tablet, or arrow keys on a computer — and step into a zone.
  2. Each zone is a body system with its own boss; the battle is a set of NCLEX-style questions.
  3. Correct answers damage the boss; wrong answers trigger its attack — read the tip after each one.
  4. Defeat the boss to clear the zone, earn cards and rewards, and unlock the next area.
  5. Work through every zone to finish the quest.
🩺

Medical Mystery game

A clinical diagnostic-reasoning simulation — you're the clinician working real patient cases inside an EHR. Interview the patient, examine, order labs and imaging, reach a diagnosis, then prove you understand the underlying pathophysiology and the nursing priorities. It's how these concepts actually show up at the bedside.

How to play
  1. Each patient arrives with a chief complaint and vitals. Use the EHR tabs to interview, examine, order labs, and request imaging — but every action costs diagnostic points, so spend wisely.
  2. When you've gathered enough evidence, submit your diagnosis from the differential. Wrong? The attending nudges you — up to 3 attempts.
  3. 🧬 Patho Mode: explain the pathophysiology in your own words; you're scored on how many key concepts you hit.
  4. 📋 NCLEX Mode: prioritize nursing interventions (what do you do FIRST?), choose the right medications, and answer an NCLEX-style question.
  5. You're graded on Diagnosis, Efficiency, and Pathophysiology — aim for "Outstanding": correct diagnosis + strong patho + efficient point use.
