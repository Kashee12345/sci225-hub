Anatomy of an Antibody

An antibody is a Y-shaped protein a plasma cell makes to lock onto one specific target. Its shape is the story: the tips vary to fit an antigen, while the stem stays constant to call in the rest of the immune system. Tap a part — on the diagram or in the list — to take it apart.
