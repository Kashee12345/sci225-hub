Endemic · Epidemic · Pandemic

Three words describe how much disease is around and where. The prefixes give it away: en-demic (always in a population), epi-demic (rising above the usual level), pan-demic (spread across all the world). Tap a level and watch the case curve.

Same disease, different wordsThese labels are about how many cases and how widespread — not how severe. A disease can be endemic in one place, flare into an epidemic when cases climb above the expected baseline, and become a pandemic once that spread crosses many countries.
