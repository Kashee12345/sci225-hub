Grow bacteria in a tube of thioglycollate broth and oxygen sorts them out for you: O₂ is richest at the surface and vanishes toward the bottom. Where a species chooses to grow reveals exactly how it handles oxygen. Tap each group to see its pattern.

Why oxygen can be lethal: using O₂ generates toxic superoxide and hydrogen peroxide. The enzymes superoxide dismutase (SOD) and catalase detoxify them — bacteria that lack these defenses are damaged or killed by air.
