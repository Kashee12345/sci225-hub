Narrow vs. Broad Spectrum

Every antibiotic covers a spectrum — the range of microbes it can kill. A narrow drug hits a small slice; a broad drug hits many kinds at once. The catch: the same drug that clears the infection also mows down the patient's protective normal flora. Pick a drug for this Group A Strep infection and watch the trade-off.
