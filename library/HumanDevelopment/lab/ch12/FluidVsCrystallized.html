Fluid vs. Crystallized Intelligence

Psychologists Raymond Cattell and John Horn split intelligence in two. Fluid intelligence is raw problem-solving power — reasoning, speed, and working memory. Crystallized intelligence is everything you've learned — vocabulary, facts, and expertise. They age in opposite directions. Drag the age slider to watch the trade-off unfold.
