Is midlife a time of crisis?For decades the "midlife crisis" was treated as an inevitable stage. Then large, long-term studies went looking for it — and mostly couldn't find it. Tap each belief to compare the popular story with what the data show.
Where the idea came from. The phrase midlife crisis was coined by psychoanalyst Elliott Jaques in 1965, then spread by Daniel Levinson's Seasons of a Man's Life (1978), which placed a "midlife transition" near age 40. But when researchers looked — most famously the MIDUS study led by Orville Brim, and work by Elaine Wethington — they found no universal crisis. Erik Erikson framed healthy midlife not as a crisis but as generativity vs. stagnation.
