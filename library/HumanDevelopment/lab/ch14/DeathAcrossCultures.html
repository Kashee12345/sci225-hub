Every culture has a “death system”Thanatologist Robert Kastenbaum observed that every society organizes death through shared people, places, times, objects, and rituals. The details vary enormously — but the functions are strikingly universal: announce the death, care for the body, dispose of the remains, support the mourners, and make meaning. Tap a tradition to compare.
Traditions vary within every culture and change over time — read these as common patterns, not rules that fit every family. What is universal is that humans everywhere mark death with ritual, tend the body, and hold the bereaved.
