Intimacy vs. Isolation

Erikson's sixth psychosocial stage lands in early adulthood — roughly ages 19 to 40 — and poses a single question: “Can I love?” Its two poles are intimacy, the capacity for close, committed relationships, and isolation, a retreat into distance and self-absorption. Drag the slider to resolve the crisis and watch the two figures — and the outcome — change.

Intimacy is not losing yourself. A healthy resolution is not total fusion. Each person keeps a firm sense of self — that is why the two coloured identity cores stay distinct even as the figures draw together.
Sixth of Erikson's eight stages. Intimacy vs. Isolation follows Identity vs. Role Confusion (adolescence) and precedes Generativity vs. Stagnation (middle adulthood). Because it builds on identity, someone still unsure of who they are often struggles with intimacy first — the stages stack.
