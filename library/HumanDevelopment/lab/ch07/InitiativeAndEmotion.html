Initiative, Guilt & Emotional Regulation

In early childhood a child becomes a planner — "I have an idea, and I can make it happen." Erikson called this the crisis of initiative vs. guilt. At the same time, preschoolers slowly learn to manage big feelings, first by borrowing a caregiver's calm and later on their own. The preschool selfBetween about 3 and 6, children take on self-chosen projects, invent games, and ask endless questions. Encouraged, they gain a sense of purpose; harshly criticized, they learn to feel guilt for wanting to act at all. Alongside this, they begin to regulate emotion — the great social-emotional task of the preschool years.
