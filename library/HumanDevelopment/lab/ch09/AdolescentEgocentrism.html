Imaginary Audience or Personal Fable?

As formal-operational thinking switches on, teens gain the power to think about what others think — and turn it on themselves. David Elkind (1967) named the two results: the imaginary audience (everyone is watching me) and the personal fable (I'm unique, and the rules don't apply to me).
