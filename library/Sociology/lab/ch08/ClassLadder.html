The Class Ladder

Ask someone their class and they will almost always answer “middle.” Sociologists need something steadier than a feeling, so they read markers — income, wealth, occupation, education, and lifestyle. Tap a rung to see what each class actually looks like, switch the shading to compare one marker across the whole ladder, then try placing eight people by their markers alone.

Five markers of class

Status inconsistency. Gerhard Lenski’s term for the common case in which the markers disagree — a plumbing contractor who out-earns a professor, a curator with a graduate degree and a modest salary. Because the markers can come apart, no single number ranks a person.
