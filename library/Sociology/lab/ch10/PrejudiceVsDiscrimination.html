Prejudice vs. Discrimination: Attitude or Action?

Everyday speech treats these as one word. Sociology does not. Prejudice is a prejudgment — an attitude about a group, held in advance of the evidence and often against it. Discrimination is unequal treatment — an act that leaves a trace in the world: a discarded résumé, a steered buyer, a denied loan. They travel together often enough that people assume they must.
