Sociology · Chapter 10

Patterns of Intergroup Relations

When a dominant group and a minority group share one society, the relationship is not improvised each morning — it settles into a pattern. Sociologists name six of them and lay them on a continuum running from intolerance to acceptance: genocide, expulsion, segregation, assimilation, amalgamation, pluralism. The same society can run different patterns toward different groups at the same time, and it can slide along the line in either direction.
