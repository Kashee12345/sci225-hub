Three Lenses on Race

A theoretical paradigm is not a guess about what happened — it is a standing set of questions you bring to any fact. Take six ordinary facts of race relations and point all three lenses at each one: what does it do for the system?, who benefits?, and how is it made real between two people? Tap a question, tap a lens, and watch the same fact turn into three different explanations. Then sort twelve theoretical claims back to the paradigm that made them.
