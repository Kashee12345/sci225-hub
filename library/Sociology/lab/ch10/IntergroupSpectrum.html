The Intergroup Spectrum: Genocide to Pluralism

When a dominant group and a minority group share a territory, sociology finds a limited menu of outcomes — and they line up on a continuum. At one end the minority is destroyed. At the other it is kept, whole and equal. In between sit expulsion, segregation, assimilation, and amalgamation.
