Formal Organizations

A formal organization is a large secondary group deliberately built to reach a goal — and there are only three reasons you are inside one. Somebody pays you, you believe in it, or someone makes you. Amitai Etzioni turned that into a typology: utilitarian, normative, and coercive. Tap the door, the tether, and the member to see how each one holds on to you — then sort nine real organizations.
