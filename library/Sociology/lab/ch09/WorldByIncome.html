The World by Income

Stratification does not stop at a border. Sort the planet by income per person and it falls into layers as steep as any class ladder inside a single country — except that here, the rung you are born on is decided almost entirely by where you were born. Tap a nation to see what its tier means, re-shade the map by life expectancy or child mortality to watch the ranking change, then sort eight countries by their indicators alone.

Why are they unequal?

About the numbers. Income tiers follow the World Bank classification by gross national income per person (Atlas method); the other figures are recent World Bank and UN estimates, rounded. They shift year to year, and a few countries sit close enough to a threshold to cross it. Treat them as the shape of the world, not as decimals.
