Through the three paradigms

Don’t confuse the two poverties. Absolute poverty is a fixed survival threshold, the same everywhere (the World Bank’s international line). Relative poverty is measured against the normal standard of one’s own society — Peter Townsend’s relative deprivation. A wealthy country can have almost no absolute poverty and a great deal of relative poverty at the same time.
