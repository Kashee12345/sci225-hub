Three Paradigms on Culture

Sociologists do not argue about what a custom is so much as about which question to ask of it. Functionalism asks what it does for the society, conflict theory asks whose interests it serves, and symbolic interactionism asks how it came to mean anything at all. They are lenses, not rivals to be settled. A full account of a custom usually needs all three: what it does for the group, whose position it protects, and what it means to the people performing it. Choosing only one is how an analysis goes thin.
