Family Forms: Who Counts as Kin

Ask a hundred people to draw their family and you will get a hundred different shapes — and almost none of them will be the one on the cereal box. Sociology treats family not as a picture but as a social institution: a durable arrangement for raising children, pooling money, and deciding who is obliged to whom. The arrangement takes wildly different forms across societies and across a single lifetime, which is the first clue that its shape is social rather than natural.
