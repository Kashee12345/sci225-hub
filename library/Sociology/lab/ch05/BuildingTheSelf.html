Building the Self

Nobody is born with a self. It has to be assembled out of other people — which is a strange thing to discover about the one thing you were sure was yours. Two theorists showed how. George Herbert Mead traced the stages a child climbs to get there; Charles Horton Cooley showed the mirror we keep checking for the rest of our lives.
