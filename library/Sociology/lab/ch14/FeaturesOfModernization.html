Features of Modernization

Modernization is the long shift from small, tradition-bound settlements to large, industrial and postindustrial societies — and it shows up in four places at once: community shrinks, choice expands, diversity grows, and time points forward. Four features at onceModernization is not one event but a bundle of changes that arrive together. Peter Berger summarized them as four: the decline of small traditional communities, the expansion of personal choice, increasing social diversity, and an orientation toward the future with a sharpened awareness of time. Berger’s four: community shrinks · choice expands · diversity grows · time points forward.
“Modern” is not a synonym for “better,” and modernization is not a ladder every society climbs in the same order. Treating one region’s history as the standard path is exactly the ethnocentrism critics have leveled at modernization theory since the 1960s.
