The Two Scales: Micro vs Macro

Sociologists study the same social world at two scales. Micro-level analysis zooms in on face-to-face interaction — individuals and small groups making meaning. Macro-level analysis zooms out to large structures — institutions, classes, and whole societies. Tap each research question, then decide which scale it belongs to.

The scale of analysis

Micro-level analysis studies small-scale, face-to-face social interaction — individuals and small groups. Macro-level analysis studies large-scale structures — institutions and whole societies.One social world, two scales of the sociological lens.
Micro vs Macrotap a question, then sort it by scale
💡 Rule of thumb: if you could watch it happen between people in one room, it’s usually micro. If it takes a whole institution or society to see the pattern, it’s macro. Symbolic interactionism works at the micro level; functionalism and conflict theory work at the macro level.
