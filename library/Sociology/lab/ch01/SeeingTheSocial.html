Free Choice, or Social Fact?

Some choices feel completely free — the name you pick, when you marry, what you eat. Yet zoom out to everyone who shares your social position and a pattern appears that no one planned. Learning to spot that hidden pattern — the social fact behind a “personal” choice — is the first skill of sociology.

Naming a baby

Social fact — Émile Durkheim (1895): a way of acting, thinking, or feeling that is external to the individual, general across the group, and quietly constrains us. It becomes visible only when we zoom out from the person to the pattern.— The Rules of Sociological Method
