Type I and Type II Errors

A test can be wrong in exactly two ways: it can cry wolf when nothing is happening, or it can sleep through something real. You choose the first error rate outright. You never choose the second one at all — it falls out of the cutoff you set, the size of the effect that is really out there, and how many observations you were willing to collect. Drag the three dials and watch the two errors refuse to move together.
