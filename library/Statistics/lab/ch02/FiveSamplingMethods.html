All five estimates are the same arithmetic — add up the sample, divide by how many. The only thing that ever changes is who ended up in the sample. In survey work the design is the whole game; the formula is an afterthought.
Strata and clusters are opposites, and they are the classic mix-up. You take a slice of every stratum, and you want each stratum to be internally alike. You take a few whole clusters, and you want each cluster to be a miniature of the entire population. Same grid, opposite goals.
