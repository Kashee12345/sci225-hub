Precision comes from n, not from N. A spoonful tells you about the pot whether the pot holds a litre or a swimming pool — provided you stirred first. Stirring is what randomness does.
Randomness is not a formality. Switch to Convenience sample and slide n up to 2,000. The estimates get tighter and tighter — around the wrong number. A biased method does not become less wrong with more data; it becomes more confidently wrong.
