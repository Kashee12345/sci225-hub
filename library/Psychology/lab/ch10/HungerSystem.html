The Hunger System

Your body doesn't decide to eat on a whim. A stream of chemical and mechanical signals — from an empty stomach, the bloodstream, the gut, fat cells, and the brain itself — pushes appetite up or reins it in. How the brain adds it up

All of these signals converge on the hypothalamus. The classic dual-center model sets a lateral "start" center against a ventromedial "stop" center, and the running balance of hunger versus satiety signals decides whether you eat. Over the long run the body defends a fairly stable weight — its set-point — nudging appetite up or down to keep energy stores roughly constant. Newer research shows the real circuitry is more of a distributed network than two tidy switches, but the start-versus-stop logic still holds.
