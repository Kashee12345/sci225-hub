Match the Method

Before running an experiment, psychologists often start by describing behavior. Three descriptive methods do the job in very different ways: a case study goes deep on one person, naturalistic observation watches behavior in the wild, and a survey asks the same questions of many people. Read each research scenario, then tap the method it uses.
