General Adaptation Syndrome

In 1936 physiologist Hans Selye noticed that wildly different stressors — cold, injury, toxins — all triggered the same three-stage bodily reaction. He named it the General Adaptation Syndrome. Drag the time marker to ride the curve through alarm, resistance, and exhaustion.

Order = ARE: Alarm → Resistance → Exhaustion. If the stressor ends during alarm or resistance the body recovers — only prolonged, unrelenting stress ever reaches exhaustion.
Eustress vs. distress: Selye distinguished eustress (motivating, manageable stress) from distress (harmful, overwhelming stress). The body climbs the same curve for both — what differs is the meaning and the dose.
