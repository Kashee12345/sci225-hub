The Four Consequences

In the 1930s B. F. Skinner showed that voluntary behavior is shaped by its consequences — an idea rooted in Edward Thorndike’s law of effect. Every consequence does just two things: it adds or removes a stimulus, and that stimulus is either pleasant or aversive. Set the two dials, and watch a rat’s lever–pressing rise or fall inside Skinner’s chamber.
