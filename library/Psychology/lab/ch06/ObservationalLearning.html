Learning by Watching

Some behaviors are never trained with our own rewards or punishments — we simply watch someone else and copy them. Albert Bandura called this observational learning, and his Bobo doll studies traced it through four steps. Step through them, then choose what happens to the model to see how consequences tip watching into doing.
