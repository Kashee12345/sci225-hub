Erikson's Stages & Attachment

Erik Erikson mapped the whole lifespan as eight psychosocial stages — each a conflict, or "crisis," between a healthy pole and an unhealthy one. Resolving each in favor of the healthy side builds a lasting strength, or virtue. Step through all eight, then tap the four infant attachment styles from Mary Ainsworth's research.

Building on John Bowlby's attachment theory, Mary Ainsworth watched infants briefly separated from and reunited with a caregiver — the "Strange Situation." Their reactions fall into four patterns.
