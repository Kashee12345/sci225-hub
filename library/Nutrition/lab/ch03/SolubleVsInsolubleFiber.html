Soluble vs Insoluble Fiber

Fiber is carbohydrate that human enzymes cannot break down, so it arrives in the large intestine essentially intact. What happens next depends on one physical property: does it dissolve in water? Soluble fiber dissolves and thickens; insoluble fiber does not and stays as particles. Walk both of them through the gut, then find out where that tidy two-bucket story starts to break down.
