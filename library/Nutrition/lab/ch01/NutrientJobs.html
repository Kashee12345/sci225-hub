What a Nutrient Does

A nutrient is a substance the body needs but cannot make in sufficient amounts, so it has to come from food. There are six classes, and each one is defined by the job it does — supplying energy, building structure, or regulating a process. Tap a class to see its job, then use the sliders to watch where a day's energy actually comes from.

Energy-yielding vs notCarbohydrate, protein, and fat are the only classes that release usable energy. Vitamins, minerals, and water release none — yet without them the energy in the other three cannot be unlocked, transported, or used. "Zero calories" is not the same as "does nothing."
