The Requirement Curve

People do not all need the same amount of a nutrient, so a single recommended number can never be the whole story. The Dietary Reference Intakes answer this by describing the spread of requirements in a group and then placing four different markers on it. Drag an intake across the curve below and watch what each marker is actually for.

EAR is the middle. RDA is the middle plus a margin. AI is what we use when there is no middle to find. UL is the ceiling. Every one of these is a population reference value used to plan and evaluate diets — none of them is a prescription, and none of them is a measurement of any individual person’s need.
