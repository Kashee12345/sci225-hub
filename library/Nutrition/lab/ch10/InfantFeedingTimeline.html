The First Year: An Infant Feeding Timeline

In twelve months an infant goes from one food to the family table. Slide through the year: watch where the day's energy actually comes from, see which developmental signs open the next door, and tap any food to find out when it usually appears — and why.

Where the day's energy comes from

Total height = the DRI Estimated Energy Requirement for healthy term infants, kcal/day. The amber band = WHO age-band estimates of the energy expected to come from complementary foods (about 200 kcal/day at 6–8 months, 300 at 9–11, 550 at 12–23), which is why it rises in steps. Milk is the remainder — and it stays the larger share for most of the first year. The millilitre figure is back-calculated from that energy at ~0.67 kcal/mL, so it is a requirement estimate; observed average intakes run somewhat lower, and a real infant self-regulates rather than hitting a number.
