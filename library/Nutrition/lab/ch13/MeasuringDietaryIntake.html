Why Intake Data Is Messy

No one can weigh a whole population's dinner. Instead we ask — and every way of asking bends the truth. The two bends that matter are random error (scatter that averages out) and systematic error (a lopsided pull that does not). Pick a method, add days of data, and watch where its estimate lands next to a person's true usual intake.
