BMI and What It Misses

Body mass index is one division problem: mass ÷ height squared. It is cheap, fast, and genuinely useful for comparing large groups. It also cannot tell muscle from fat, cannot see where tissue sits, and was never built to describe an individual. Compute it, then split it apart and watch it misclassify.

BMI is a screening ratio for populations, not a diagnosis and not a description of a person. The WHO bands shown here apply to adults aged 20 and over; children and adolescents are assessed against age-and-sex percentile charts instead, and the bands are not interpretable during pregnancy or with limb loss, large fluid shifts, or significant edema or ascites. Nothing here is medical or dietary advice, no food is labelled good or bad, and no number on this page says anything about what a body should look like.
