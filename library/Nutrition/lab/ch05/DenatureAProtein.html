🔒 Denaturation never breaks peptide bonds. The order of amino acids — the primary structure — is untouched. Only hydrolysis (digestion) cuts the backbone. So a cooked protein still contains every amino acid the raw one did, and still supplies 4 kcal per gram (carbohydrate 4, fat 9, alcohol 7).

Does unfolding change the protein's value?

Strong evidenceIllustrative model
Read this carefully. Unfolding a protein makes its backbone easier for enzymes to reach, and for egg that effect has been measured: true ileal digestibility of egg protein is about 51% raw and about 91% cooked in healthy adults. That is a strong, specific finding about egg — it is not a general rule that any preparation method is better, and no single food or cooking style causes or cures disease. The live pointer above is a teaching model, not a food-specific measurement. Foods are not "good" or "bad" here; this is chemistry.
