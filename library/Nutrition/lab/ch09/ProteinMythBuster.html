Protein: The Claims vs the Evidence

Almost everyone repeats a rule about protein — a number per meal, a magic window, a warning about kidneys. Set a body mass to see the actual reference values, then tap each claim to see where it lands against the evidence.

Reading the values. The RDA of 0.8 g protein per kg body mass per day is set for healthy adults (ages 19+) and is meant to meet the needs of about 97.5% of that group. The EAR (0.66 g/kg/day) is the median requirement. These scale with body mass because tissue upkeep does — they are population references, not a target for any one person, and this page is not medical or dietary advice.

Strong vs preliminarySome of these claims restate an official reference value; others are well-supported for athletes; several are overstated or preliminary, where the research is mixed. Tap a claim to see which — and, where a claim implies a number, watch it drop onto the chart.
Energy note. Protein yields 4 kcal/g (carbohydrate 4, fat 9, alcohol 7). The AMDR — the acceptable range for adults — is 10–35% of total energy from protein, which is a share of the whole day's intake, not a per-kilogram figure.
