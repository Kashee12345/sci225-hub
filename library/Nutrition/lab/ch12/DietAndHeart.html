Diet & Heart Disease

Diet doesn't act on the heart directly — it nudges risk markers like blood cholesterol, blood pressure, and triglycerides. Move each dietary factor and watch the markers respond. The shifts shown are average, illustrative effects seen in nutrition research, not a prediction for any one person.

This is a teaching model, not medical advice. No single food causes or cures heart disease; risk reflects whole eating patterns, genetics, activity, sleep, medicines, and access to care. These sliders show directions from population research, not therapeutic doses or a personal forecast. For your own health decisions, talk with your clinician.
Reference intakes (adults): sodium Adequate Intake 1,500 mg/day, with a Chronic Disease Risk Reduction intake of 2,300 mg/day; potassium AI 3,400 mg/day (men) / 2,600 mg/day (women); total fiber AI ~38 g/day (men) / 25 g/day (women) — viscous fiber is one part of that total.
