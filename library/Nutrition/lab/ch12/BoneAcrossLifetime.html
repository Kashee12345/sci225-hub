Building & Losing Bone Across a Lifetime

Bone is living tissue: we build it fastest in childhood and adolescence, bank a peak around age 30, then slowly remodel and lose it with age. Set a person's habitual calcium, vitamin D, and weight-bearing activity, then drag the age scrubber to walk the whole curve. The shapes shown are average, illustrative trends — genetics set most of the peak, and no single number predicts any one body.

This is a teaching model, not medical advice. It does not diagnose bone health, prescribe supplement doses, or predict fractures. Real bone density depends heavily on genetics, plus body size, hormones, medicines, medical conditions, smoking, and more. Calcium and vitamin D above adequacy do not keep adding density in someone already replete, and very high supplement doses carry their own risks. For your own bones, talk with a clinician.
Reference intakes (per day): Calcium RDA 1,000 mg for adults 19–50 and men 51–70; 1,200 mg for women 51+ and everyone 71+ (Upper Limit 2,000–2,500 mg). Vitamin D RDA 600 IU (15 µg) for ages 1–70; 800 IU (20 µg) for 71+ (Upper Limit 4,000 IU). Weight-bearing activity means load-bearing movement such as walking, stairs, or resistance work.
