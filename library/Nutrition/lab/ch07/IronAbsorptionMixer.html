The Iron Absorption Mixer

Iron reaches the gut in two chemically different forms. Heme iron — locked inside a porphyrin ring in meat, poultry and fish — enters through its own carrier and mostly ignores the rest of the meal. Non-heme iron — every other source — must be reduced and kept dissolved first, so everything eaten alongside it gets a vote. Set the two iron dials, flip the enhancers and inhibitors, and watch how many milligrams actually cross into the blood.

No food here is "good" or "bad," and nothing on this page is advice for any individual. Phytate, polyphenols and calcium simply bind iron inside the gut — and the very same foods deliver fiber, folate, calcium and protein the body uses elsewhere. This is a teaching model of one meal's chemistry, built from published single-meal absorption studies. Real absorption varies enormously between people and between days, it cannot be read off a menu, and questions about testing, supplements or doses belong with a person's own clinician.
