Before You Give Digoxin

Digoxin has a razor-thin therapeutic window, and the four things that decide whether it helps or harms are all nursing assessments — the apical pulse, the serum level, the potassium, and what the patient tells you. Set the findings and see what the nurse does.

What this patient needs to hear

Digoxin is renally cleared with a 36–48 hour half-life — a rising creatinine, a new loop diuretic, or added amiodarone, verapamil, or quinidine will push the level up days later.
