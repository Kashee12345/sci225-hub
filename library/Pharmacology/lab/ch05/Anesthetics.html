Anesthetics: Stages & Nerve Blockade

Two very different jobs. A general anesthetic shuts the brain down in an orderly sequence you can read at the bedside. A local anesthetic never reaches the brain at all — it stops the signal at the nerve, and the patient stays wide awake. Slide a patient down through the stages, then explore the block.
