Antiepileptics: Calming the Neuron

A seizure is a population of neurons firing too much, too fast, together. Every antiepileptic drug fixes that in one of two ways — it lifts off the gas (block Na+, Ca2+, or glutamate) or it presses the brake (boost GABA). Learn the four targets, then the drugs that hit them, then the numbers you draw. Pick a mode.
