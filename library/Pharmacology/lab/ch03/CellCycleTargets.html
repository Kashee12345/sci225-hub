The Cell Cycle & Where Chemo Strikes

Antineoplastics don't kill cancer cells — they kill dividing cells. Where a drug lands on the cell cycle predicts what it destroys, which healthy tissue it wrecks, and what you monitor. Tap a phase on the wheel, or tap a drug class to see where it lands.

Growth fraction is everythingChemotherapy hits cells that are actively cycling. A tumor with a high growth fraction (leukemias, lymphomas, testicular cancer) melts away; a slow, bulky solid tumor sitting mostly in G0 barely flinches. The same rule explains the toxicity profile: your bone marrow, GI mucosa, and hair follicles divide fastest, so they get hurt first — myelosuppression, mucositis, and alopecia.
