The Nephron: Where Each Diuretic Works

A diuretic has exactly one idea: stop the tubule from taking sodium back, because water follows sodium out the door. There are only four sodium doors along the tube — so knowing which door a drug jams tells you how much fluid it can pull, what it does to potassium and calcium, and which lab you check before you give it.
