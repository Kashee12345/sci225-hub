Three Ways Things Move

Karl Polanyi argued that in every society goods travel along one of three paths, and that you can tell them apart by the shape of the flow rather than by what is being moved. Things go back and forth between two parties who know each other; or they go in to a centre and out again; or they change hands at a price between people who need no relationship at all. Learn the three shapes, then sort twelve real transactions into them.

The three shapes

Three questions that tell them apart

  • 1Do the two sides stand in a lasting social relationship — kin, partners, neighbours — that the transfer is meant to keep alive? Then it is reciprocity.
  • 2Does everything pass through a centre — a big man, a chief, a temple, a treasury — before any of it comes back out? Then it is redistribution.
  • 3Was the rate set by what others would pay, with no relationship required before or after? Then it is market exchange.
Two hands · a hub · a price. Polanyi called these forms of integration, not stages of history. Every society runs all three at once; what differs is which one carries the bulk of the goods, and whether land and labour themselves are priced.
