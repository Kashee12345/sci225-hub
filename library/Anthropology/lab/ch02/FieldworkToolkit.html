The Fieldwork Toolkit

Ethnography is not one method but a kit. Ask a question four different ways — in an interview, on a genealogy, on a map, across a whole life — and four different kinds of data come back. Open each instrument to see what it yields, where it is blind, and how it has actually been used in the field.

No instrument is complete — which is why there are four. Fieldworkers triangulate: they check what an interview claims against what a map shows, what a genealogy predicts against what a life history remembers. And all four sit inside participant observation — the long co-residence Bronisław Malinowski made the discipline’s signature. The instruments collect the data; living there is what tells you which data to trust.
