The Observer Effect

There is no scene you can watch without joining it. The moment an ethnographer walks in, people begin doing the version of their lives they would like a visitor to carry away. Choose a setting, choose how you are present, and watch how long it takes the ordinary to come back — and whether it ever fully does.

Labov’s observer’s paradox: the goal is to find out how people behave when they are not being observed — and the only way to get the data is to observe them. You cannot solve it. You can only shrink it, and then write down that it was there.
Reflexivity is the discipline’s answer. You cannot subtract yourself from the scene, so you write yourself into the account. That is why modern ethnographies say I — not as confession, but so the reader can see the instrument.
