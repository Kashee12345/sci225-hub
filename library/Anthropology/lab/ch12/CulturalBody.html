Before you startMedical anthropology treats biomedicine as one system among many, not as the referee that grades the others. Each model below explains, predicts and treats — and each is used by people alive today.
Emic & etic: an emic account explains a body model in the terms its own users would recognise; an etic account compares models from outside. Franz Boas asked us to do the emic work first — including for our own medicine.
Common error: calling these “beliefs” layered over a real, universal body. Present tense matters too — Ayurveda, East Asian medicine, Unani and Diné ceremony are living, practised systems, not survivals.
