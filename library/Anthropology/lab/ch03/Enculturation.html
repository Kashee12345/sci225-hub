Enculturation: How a Child Absorbs Culture

No one is born with a culture, and no one chooses one. Children absorb the culture around them — its sounds, its kin terms, its manners, its idea of what a person is — through a lifelong process anthropologists call enculturation. Clifford Geertz described human beings as unfinished animals who complete themselves through culture; this is that completion, happening.
