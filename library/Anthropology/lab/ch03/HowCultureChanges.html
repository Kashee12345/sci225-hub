How Culture Changes

No culture stands still, and every culture is largely made of things it did not invent. Anthropologists sort culture change by where it came from: born inside a society (innovation), carried in from another society at a distance (diffusion), or worked out between two societies locked in sustained contact (acculturation). Globalization is not erasure. Marshall Sahlins argued that people put imported goods to work saying local things — the indigenization of modernity. Arjun Appadurai described culture moving in global flows rather than radiating from a single centre. Contact reliably changes cultures. It does not reliably dissolve them.
