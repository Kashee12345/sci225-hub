Why anthropology gets hiredEvery case below began the same way: an institution had a plan that made sense on paper and failed with real people. The anthropologist's contribution is rarely a new fact — it is the emic view, the situation as the people living it understand it, gathered by participant observation before anyone proposes a fix.
The core skill is old. Malinowski argued in 1922 that the point of long fieldwork is to grasp “the native's point of view” — and Boas insisted that a practice be judged inside its own culture's terms rather than against the observer's, the stance we now call cultural relativism. Applied work simply carries that discipline into a clinic, a courtroom, a construction site, or a design studio.
Applied work has clients, and clients have interests. The AAA Code of Ethics puts one obligation ahead of the funder, the agency and the anthropologist's own career: do no harm to the people studied. Informed consent, the right to remain anonymous, and returning findings to the community are not paperwork — they are the line between collaboration and extraction. The discipline learned this from projects that were quietly turned into intelligence and counterinsurgency work.
