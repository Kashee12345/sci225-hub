Anthropology · Chapter 8

Person, Policy, System

Since Boas, anthropology has held that racial categories are made by societies rather than found in biology — the American Anthropological Association said so plainly in its 1998 Statement on Race. So the useful question is not what race someone is, but where the sorting happens. It happens at three levels, and mistaking one for another is the most common error in any argument about racism. Learn the three tests, then sort nine real cases.
