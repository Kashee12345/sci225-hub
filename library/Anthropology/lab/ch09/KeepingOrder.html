Keeping Order

Every society has to answer the same question: what happens when someone breaks a rule? The whole answer is called social control, and anthropologists sort it by who does the sanctioning. Informal sanctions are diffuse — anyone may apply them, no rule is written down, and they work through reputation. Formal law is organized — a recognized authority applies a penalty, and its right to do so is not in dispute. Read each scene, make the call, and watch where it lands on the spectrum.
