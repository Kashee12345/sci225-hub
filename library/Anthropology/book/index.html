Cultural Anthropology

humankind, explained plainly

An introduction to cultural anthropology — the study of how people everywhere make meaning, kinship, power, ritual, and art. Told in plain English with real ethnographic examples, comparison tables, insight boxes, and a self-check in every chapter.

by Dr. Vidya Aggrawal · Nightingale College
🌏 A note: the book runs 14 chapters — from the four fields and fieldwork through culture, language, subsistence, kinship, gender, race, politics, religion, art, health, globalization, and applied anthropology. Each chapter explains the idea plainly, shows a real figure, and ends with a self-check.
