The definition. A number is in scientific notation when it reads a × 10n with 1 ≤ |a| < 10 and n an integer. Exactly one nonzero digit in front of the point — no more, no fewer.
The trap. A negative exponent does not make a negative number. 3 × 10−4 = 0.0003, which is positive. The exponent's sign says which way the point moved; the coefficient's sign says whether the number is negative. The two are unrelated.
