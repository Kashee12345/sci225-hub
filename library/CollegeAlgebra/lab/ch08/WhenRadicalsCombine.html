When Radicals Combine

There is one asymmetry at the heart of this whole section, and everything else follows from it: multiplication passes through a radical sign, and addition does not. √2 · √3 really is √6, but √2 + √3 is not √5 — it is 3.146264, while √5 is only 2.236068. Adding radicals is therefore never about the numbers inside; it is about counting matching units, exactly like 5x + 2x.
