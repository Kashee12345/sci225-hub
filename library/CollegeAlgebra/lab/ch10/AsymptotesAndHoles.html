Asymptotes and Holes: Where the Graph Breaks

A rational function is undefined wherever the bottom is zero — but those spots are not all alike. Some are walls the graph runs along forever; some are a single missing point you would never notice by looking. The factored form tells you which is which, and this walks you through the check one step at a time.

The three tests, in one place

Vertical asymptoteAfter cancelling, a value that makes the bottom zero while the top is not zero. A nonzero number over something shrinking to 0 grows without bound. The graph can never cross it.
HoleA factor that appears on top and bottom and cancels. Substituting gives 0/0. Height of the hole = plug the value into the simplified form.
Horizontal asymptoteCompare degrees. Bottom bigger → y = 0. Degrees equal → y = ratio of leading coefficients. Top bigger → none (one bigger gives a slant line). The graph may cross it.
