Rise Over Run

Slope is one number answering one question: as you walk to the right along the line, how fast does it climb? Count the vertical change — the rise — count the horizontal change — the run — and divide. Drag either point below and watch the triangle, the fraction, and the line redraw together.

Two points make a slope

Why any two points give the same answer

Here is the reason the definition is allowed to exist. Pick a far-apart pair instead of a close pair and you build a bigger right triangle on the very same line — but it is the same shape, just scaled up. Both legs grow by the identical factor (here ×3), and multiplying top and bottom of a fraction by the same number never changes its value: 3 · 1 over 3 · 3 is still 1 over 3. So slope belongs to the line, not to the pair of points you happened to pick.
