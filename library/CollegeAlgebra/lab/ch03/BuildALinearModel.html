Why square the misses? A miss above the line and a miss below it would cancel each other if we just added them up — a terrible line could score zero. Squaring makes every miss positive, and it punishes one big blunder far more than several small ones. The best-fit line is the single line that makes the total of those squares as small as it can possibly be.

Snap to it and you will notice it is close to, but not exactly, the line built from two points in the other tab. That is expected: a two-point model hears only two measurements, while the best-fit line listens to all five at once.
