Making a Variable Disappear

Elimination has exactly one idea in it: rescale the equations until one column is equal and opposite, then add. That column collapses to zero and you are left with one equation in one unknown. Choose the multipliers yourself below and watch the column die — then walk the whole solve, one step at a time.

The cancel bench

Why either move is legal

Multiplying a whole equation by a number that is not zero changes nothing that matters. 3x + 4y = 18 and 6x + 8y = 36 are true for exactly the same pairs (x, y), because you can divide back by 2 and return. Same line, new clothes.

Adding two true equations is just adding equals to equals: if A = B and C = D, then A + C = B + D. Anything that satisfied both equations still satisfies the sum.

The multiplier may never be 0. Multiplying by 0 turns an equation into 0 = 0 — true for every point in the plane. You would not have simplified the system, you would have thrown one equation away.
