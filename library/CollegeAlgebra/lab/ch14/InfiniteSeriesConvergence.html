When Does an Infinite Sum Converge?

An infinite geometric series keeps adding terms forever: a + ar + ar2 + ar3 + ···. Sometimes the running total settles on a single number; sometimes it races off to infinity. The whole outcome hinges on one value — the ratio r. Drag it across 1 and watch.

Running total after each term

Each dot is a partial sum — the total after that many terms. The dashed line is the limit the totals chase.
