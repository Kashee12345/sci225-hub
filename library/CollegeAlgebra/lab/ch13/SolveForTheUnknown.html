Why a logarithm is the tool. The unknown t lives upstairs, in the exponent, so adding and dividing can never reach it. A logarithm is the one operation that pulls an exponent down to the ground: log(bx) = x·log b. Once t is a plain coefficient the equation is linear, and linear equations we already know how to finish.

What are we solving for?

Read the question before you reach for the log. If the ratio of new to old is a tidy power — 2, 4, 8, ½, ¼ — both sides share a base and matching exponents finishes it with no calculator. Only when the ratio is untidy (0.30, 0.62, 1.07…) does the logarithm earn its keep.
