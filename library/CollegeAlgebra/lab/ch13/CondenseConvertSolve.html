Condense, Convert, Solve

A logarithmic equation hides the unknown inside a logarithm. The escape route never changes: condense the log side down to a single logarithm, convert that one logarithm into exponential form, then solve what is left. One extra duty comes with the territory — a logarithm refuses negative and zero inputs, so every candidate must be checked in the original equation before you are allowed to call it a solution.
