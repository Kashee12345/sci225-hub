Undoing the Rectangle

Multiplying builds a rectangle out of pieces. Factoring runs the film backwards — you are handed the pieces and asked to rebuild the rectangle. Two moves do almost all the work: pull out the greatest common factor (find the shared height), and, when four terms have nothing in common, factor by grouping (find the shared width).
