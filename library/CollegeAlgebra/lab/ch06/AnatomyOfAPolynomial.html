Anatomy of a Polynomial

Before you can factor one, you have to be able to read one. Three questions do almost all the work: what is the highest power (the degree), what number rides on that highest power (the leading coefficient), and how many pieces are joined by plus and minus signs (the number of terms). Move the sliders and watch all three answers — and the graph — change together.
