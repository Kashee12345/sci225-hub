perfect squares1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49, 64, 81, 100, 121, 144, 169, 196, 225 perfect cubes1, 8, 27, 64, 125, 216, 343, 512, 729, 1000 A variable power is a square when its exponent is even — halve it: √(x10) = x5. It is a cube when the exponent divides by 3 — take a third: the cube root of x12 is x4. 64 is both (82 and 43), so x6 − 64 can be opened either way. Start with the difference of squares — it factors further.

SOAP keeps the cube signs straight. Reading (a ± b)(a2 ∓ ab + b2) left to right: Same sign as the original, then the Opposite sign, then Always Positive.
The trap: a sum of squares does not factor over the real numbers. x2 + 9, 4x2 + 25 and x2 + 1 are prime — leave them alone. Cubes are different: both a3 + b3 and a3 − b3 factor. And always pull out a common factor first: 2x2 − 18 is not a difference of squares until you write it as 2(x2 − 9).
