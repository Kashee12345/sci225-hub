Gallstones & the Biliary Tree

Where the stone lodges = which disease · Pathophysiology Week 14
Gallstones (mostly cholesterol + bile acids) crystallize in the gallbladder when bile sits still. Sitting there, they\'re usually silent. The trouble starts when a stone moves and lodges in a duct — and which duct decides the whole picture. Click each spot.

The biliary tree

Liver → ducts → gallbladder, all draining down to the duodenum. The pancreatic duct joins right at the end.

What happens

The classic risk profile — the 4 F\'s: Female, Forty (age >40), Fat (obesity/high cholesterol/high-fat diet), Fertile (multiple pregnancies). Also: rapid weight loss/dieting, prolonged TPN, and hormone replacement.
