Gas exchange needs two things to line up at the alveolus: Ventilation (V) — air getting in — and Perfusion (Q) — blood flowing past. When one is missing you get a V/Q mismatch: shunt (blood, no air) or dead space (air, no blood). Click each to see the alveolus.

What's happening

The three components of respiration

Ventilation

getting air in & out of the alveoli. Blocked by asthma, COPD, OSA, CF.

Diffusion

gas crossing the alveolar-capillary wall. Blocked when alveoli fill with fluid — pneumonia, edema, ARDS.

Perfusion

blood flowing to the alveoli. Blocked by a pulmonary embolism or pulmonary HTN.
Memory trick: V/Q — Shunt keeps the blood but Stops the air (perfusion without ventilation); Dead space keeps the air but Drops the blood (ventilation without perfusion).
