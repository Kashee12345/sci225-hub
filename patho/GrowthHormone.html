Growth Hormone Disorders

Timing is everything · Pathophysiology Week 8
Growth hormone (somatotropin) drives bone, muscle, and tissue growth. Too little stunts growth; too much makes things grow abnormally — and whether the growth plates are still open decides which disorder you get. Click each.

What it is

The key question: are the growth plates open? Excess GH before the plates close → the whole skeleton grows → gigantism (very tall). Excess GH after they close → bones can only thicken, not lengthen → acromegaly (big hands, feet, jaw, brow). Both usually come from a GH-secreting pituitary adenoma.
