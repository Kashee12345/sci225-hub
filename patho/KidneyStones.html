A kidney stone forms when urine gets too concentrated. Here's the twist students miss: a stone sitting in the kidney often causes no pain — the agony only starts when it drops into the narrow ureter. And if it gets stuck, urine backs up and swells the kidney (hydronephrosis). Click each stage.

Risk & prevention

Risk factors: hypercalcemia (hyperparathyroidism / parathyroid tumor), high uric acid, and dehydration.  Prevention is simple: drink plenty of water — dilute urine keeps crystals from clumping into stones and helps flush small ones out.
