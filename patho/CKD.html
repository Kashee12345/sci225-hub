Chronic Kidney Disease

Drag the GFR down and watch the whole body change · Pathophysiology Week 11
In CKD the kidneys are slowly, permanently damaged, so the GFR falls and waste and fluid build up. But the kidney does more than filter — it also makes EPO, activates vitamin D, and balances acid & electrolytes. Lower the GFR below and watch every one of those jobs fail.

Most common causes: Diabetes and Hypertension — the same two that damage every other blood vessel.  Diet in CKD: low salt & potassium, limit magnesium laxatives, and take vitamin D + calcium supplements.

Why patients feel awful (the consequences)

Anemia & fatigue — the failing kidney makes less erythropoietin (EPO), so the marrow makes fewer red cells.
Weak bones (renal osteodystrophy) — the kidney can't activate vitamin D, so calcium drops and phosphate rises.
Metabolic acidosis — the kidney can't excrete H⁺, so acid builds up in the blood.
Hyperkalemia — potassium isn't excreted → dangerous heart arrhythmias.
Fluid overload — salt & water are retained → edema, hypertension, and strain on the heart.
