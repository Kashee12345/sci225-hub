Hypersensitivity Reactions

When the immune system overreacts · Pathophysiology Week 2
A hypersensitivity reaction is the immune system overreacting to an antigen — the body is damaged by its own response, not by the trigger itself. There are four types; remember them with ACID. Click each.

What happens

Related ideas: autoimmunity = the immune system attacks its own body (family history, women > men). Immunodeficiency = the immune system is weakened (HIV, cancer/chemo, diabetes, malnutrition, splenectomy, stress). Both differ from hypersensitivity, where the system is too active against an outside trigger.
