When the air sacs fill up or collapse · Pathophysiology Week 12
Oxygen has to cross from the alveolus into the blood — that's diffusion. If the sac fills with fluid or pus, or collapses, the oxygen can't get across. These are the "diffusion problem" diseases. Click each and watch whether O₂ makes it into the blood.

What's happening

Same end result, different cause: pneumonia fills sacs with pus (infection, one area), pulmonary edema fills them with watery fluid (backed-up heart), ARDS floods both lungs with inflammatory fluid (sepsis/trauma), and atelectasis collapses them (blockage/post-op). All block diffusion → hypoxia. On exam you'd hear crackles.
