Fluid Regulation Hormones

RAAS · ADH · ANP/BNP · Pathophysiology Week 1
The body watches blood volume like a thermostat. When volume/BP drops too low, it turns on hormones that retain salt and water (RAAS, ADH). When volume runs too high, the stretched heart releases hormones that dump salt and water (ANP/BNP). Flip the volume and watch who responds.

What it does

Simple rule: low volume → RAAS + ADH turn ON to retain (aldosterone keeps salt & water, ADH keeps water). High volume → the stretched heart fires ANP/BNP to dump salt & water. RAAS/ADH and ANP/BNP are opposites.
