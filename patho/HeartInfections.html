The one-liner map

Three layers, three "-itis": pericarditis = the sac (sharp chest pain, friction rub), myocarditis = the muscle (weak pump → failure/arrhythmia), endocarditis = the lining & valves (bacteria + vegetations + emboli). Rheumatic heart disease is the odd one out — not a live infection, but an autoimmune attack on the valves after strep throat.
