HIV — a special case: HIV is a retrovirus that targets CD4 (T-helper) cells — the very cells that coordinate immunity. As it destroys them, the immune system collapses, letting opportunistic infections take hold (TB, oral thrush, CNS toxoplasmosis) and cancers like Kaposi sarcoma — that's AIDS.
