Blood Types & Transfusion

ABO / Rh compatibility & transfusion reactions · Pathophysiology Week 9
Red cells carry antigens on their surface (A, B, and the Rh "D" antigen). Your plasma carries antibodies against the antigens you don't have. Give someone red cells their antibodies recognize and the cells clump and burst. Pick a recipient and a donor and see what happens.

Transfusion reactions

🚨 Acute Hemolytic Reaction
ABO incompatibility — the worst one. Recipient IgM antibodies destroy donor cells within ~15 minutes: fever, chills, low BP, back pain, dark urine (hemoglobinuria). Can trigger DIC and acute renal failure.
Febrile Reaction
Against donor white cells (leukocyte incompatibility). Fever & chills, but no hemolysis — uncomfortable, not usually dangerous.
Fluid Overload
Too much volume, too fast → strains the heart (CHF), especially in the elderly.
Infection / Sepsis
Contaminated blood introduces bacteria → sepsis.
