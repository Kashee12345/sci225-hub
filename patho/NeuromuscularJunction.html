Neuromuscular Junction Diseases

Three ways to break the nerve-muscle handshake · Pathophysiology Week 4
Muscles move when a nerve releases acetylcholine (ACh), which crosses a tiny gap and binds receptors on the muscle. Three diseases each sabotage a different point in that handshake — and that's why one causes floppy paralysis, one causes rigid spasms, and one causes weakness. Click each.

What's broken

Contrast the three: Botulism blocks ACh release → floppy (flaccid), descending paralysis. Tetanus blocks the inhibitory "brake" in the CNS → rigid spasms (lockjaw). Myasthenia gravis destroys ACh receptors → fatigable weakness (worse with use, better with rest).
