Chest Trauma

When the chest wall or pleura is breached · Pathophysiology Week 12
The lungs stay inflated because they're sealed inside the pleural space — two smooth layers with a vacuum between them. Break that seal (air, blood, or crushed ribs) and the lung collapses. One version — tension pneumothorax — is a true emergency. Click each injury.
