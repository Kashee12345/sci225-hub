Adrenal Disorders

Too much vs too little cortisol · Pathophysiology Week 8
The adrenal glands sit on the kidneys. The cortex makes corticosteroids (cortisol, aldosterone). Too much = Cushing's; too little = Addison's. The medulla makes adrenaline — a tumor there is a pheochromocytoma. Click each.

What's happening

Never stop steroids abruptly! Long-term oral steroids shut off the body's own cortisol production. Stopping suddenly leaves a gap before natural production restarts → an Addisonian crisis. Always taper. (Feedback: an adrenal tumor making its own cortisol drives CRH and ACTH down; a failed adrenal gland drives them up.)
