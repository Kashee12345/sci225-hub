How much blood the heart moves per minute (cardiac output) is just how much it pushes each beat (stroke volume) times how fast it beats (heart rate). Stroke volume itself depends on three dials — preload, afterload, and contractility. Play with them and watch cardiac output and blood pressure respond.

How the body regulates BP

↑ RAAS — the "raise BP" system
When blood flow to the kidney drops, it releases renin → angiotensin II (a strong vasoconstrictor, raises afterload) → aldosterone (kidneys keep salt & water, raises preload/volume). ADH holds water too. Net effect: BP up.
↓ ANP / BNP — the "lower BP" system
When the heart chambers are over-stretched by too much volume, they release ANP & BNP → arteries dilate and the kidneys dump salt & water. Net effect: BP down. (BNP is also the classic heart-failure blood test.)
