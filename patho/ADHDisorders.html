ADH Disorders

SIADH vs Diabetes Insipidus · Pathophysiology Week 8
ADH (antidiuretic hormone) tells the kidney to hold on to water. These two disorders are exact opposites: too much ADH (SIADH) makes you retain water, while too little ADH (Diabetes Insipidus) makes you dump it. Click each.

What's happening

SIADH (too much)

Retain water → diluted blood, LOW sodium, concentrated urine, fluid overload, weight gain.

DI (too little)

Lose water → dehydration, HIGH sodium, huge volumes of dilute urine, intense thirst.
