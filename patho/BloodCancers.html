Leukemia & lymphoma · Pathophysiology Week 2
White-blood-cell cancers come in two big families: leukemia (cancer of the early blood cells in the marrow/blood) and lymphoma (cancer of the lymphatic system / lymph nodes). Pick one, then compare the sub-types.

Where it grows

Leukemia floods the blood with abnormal cells; lymphoma builds masses in lymph nodes.

What it is

Lymphoma memory hook: Hodgkin is the "nicer" one — localized, spreads contiguously (node to next node), has Reed-Sternberg cells, hits younger adults, and has a better prognosis. Non-Hodgkin is diffuse/scattered, jumps around (non-contiguous, extranodal), hits older adults, worse prognosis.
