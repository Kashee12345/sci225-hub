Shunts & the "blue baby" · Septal defects · PDA · Tetralogy · Pathophysiology
A shunt is a hole or extra pipe that lets blood take a shortcut between the two sides of the heart. Which way it flows is set by pressure — and that decides everything. Left → Right sends oxygen-rich blood back to the lungs — the baby stays pink (non-cyanotic). Right → Left sends oxygen-poor blood out to the body — the baby turns blue (cyanotic).

The defect & the shortcut

The left heart is higher pressure than the right, so blood usually shortcuts left→right — unless something (like Tetralogy) forces it the other way.

The big picture

Every defect here is one of two kinds — and the direction tells you if it's a blue baby.

◾ Left → Right · PINK

VSD, ASD, PDA
Oxygen-rich blood recirculates to the lungs. Extra lung/heart work, but no cyanosis.

◾ Right → Left · BLUE

Tetralogy of Fallot
Oxygen-poor blood bypasses the lungs to the body → cyanosis (blue baby).
The one rule that ties it together: follow the blood at the hole. If red (oxygen-rich) blood is being pushed to the right side → it just goes to the lungs → pink. If blue (oxygen-poor) blood crosses to the left side / aorta → it goes to the body → blue.
