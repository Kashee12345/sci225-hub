Asthma — Attack & Rescue

Why the airway slams shut · Pathophysiology Week 12
Asthma is a reversible narrowing of the airways with two problems at once: the muscle around the tube squeezes shut (bronchoconstriction) and the lining gets inflamed and mucus-filled. A trigger sets it off; a rescue inhaler opens it back up. Click through the attack.

What's happening

1 · Bronchoconstriction

The smooth muscle ring around the airway tightens and squeezes the tube narrower.

2 · Inflammation

The lining swells and pumps out mucus, clogging what little opening is left.
Signs: dyspnea & chest tightness, wheezing, and cough (classically worse at night). Because it's reversible, a bronchodilator (rescue inhaler) relaxes the muscle and reopens the airway.
