Stroke (CVA)

Two ways the brain loses blood · Pathophysiology Week 5
A stroke is a sudden loss of blood flow to part of the brain. It happens two ways: ischemic — a clot blocks a vessel (80–85% of strokes) — or hemorrhagic — a vessel ruptures and bleeds (15–20%). Click each.

What's happening

Spot a stroke — FAST: Face drooping, Arm weakness (one-sided), Speech change, Time to call. A sudden severe "worst headache" + vomiting + loss of consciousness leans hemorrhagic. Bell's palsy also droops the face — but it's only the face (CN 7), with no arm/leg or speech-comprehension involvement.
