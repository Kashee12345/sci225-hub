Pulmonary Embolism

A leg clot that lands in the lung · Pathophysiology Week 12
A PE is a clot that forms somewhere else — usually a deep vein in the leg (DVT) — then breaks loose, rides the bloodstream up through the heart, and lodges in a pulmonary artery. Air still reaches those alveoli, but no blood can get past the clot to pick it up — dead space. Follow the clot.

What's happening

Risk factors

  • Deep vein thrombosis
  • Immobility / long trips
  • Femur fracture, surgery
  • Birth control pills
  • Smoking, obesity

Signs & symptoms

  • Sudden shortness of breath
  • Chest pain
  • Hemoptysis (coughing blood)
  • Hypoxemia (low O₂)
  • Tachycardia
Why it drops O₂: the blocked lung zone is ventilated but not perfused — classic dead space. Meanwhile blood is forced through the remaining lung too fast to fully oxygenate, so oxygen falls.
