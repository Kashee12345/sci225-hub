Heart Valve Disorders

Stenosis vs Regurgitation · Mitral & Aortic · Pathophysiology · Cardiovascular Part 2
Think of each valve as a one-way door: it OPENS to let blood through, then SHUTS so blood can't fall back. Only two things go wrong — the door gets stiff and won't OPEN (stenosis), or it won't latch and leaks backward (regurgitation). The catch is timing: each door is only used during one part of the heartbeat, so that's when its problem shows up.

Watch the valve through the cardiac cycle

Systolic vs diastolic — the whole set

Every valve lesion is either a systolic or a diastolic murmur. Here's all four:

◾ Systolic murmurs

Aortic Stenosis — LV can't eject
Mitral Regurgitation — LV leaks back to LA

◾ Diastolic murmurs

Aortic Regurgitation — aorta leaks back to LV
Mitral Stenosis — LV can't fill
Memory hook: a valve makes a murmur when it's supposed to be shut but isn't (regurg) or supposed to be open but won't (stenosis). Aortic opens in systole, mitral opens in diastole — so AS & MR are systolic; AR & MS are diastolic.
