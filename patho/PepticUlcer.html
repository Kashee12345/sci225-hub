When acid eats the lining · Pathophysiology Week 13
The stomach protects itself from its own acid with a mucus coat. Wreck that coat — usually with H. pylori bacteria or NSAIDs — and acid digs in: first inflammation (gastritis), then an open sore (ulcer). The trick that tells gastric from duodenal? What's happening

Pain-timing rule: Gastric ulcer → pain Gets worse when you eat (more acid + churning on the sore). Duodenal ulcer → pain when the stomach is empty (often at night) and eating relieves it. #1 cause of both = H. pylori; NSAIDs/ASA/steroids are close behind.
