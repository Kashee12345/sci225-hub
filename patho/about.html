About this resource

What is this?

A pathophysiology course you can play with

The Interactive Body is a free collection of 86 hands-on, animated lessons that cover an entire pathophysiology course — every body system, from fluids & acid-base to genetics, endocrine, and beyond.

Instead of reading a slide, you interact: click through a viral replication cycle, drag the intracranial pressure up until Cushing's triad appears, hear real lung sounds, or type a blood gas into a calculator and watch it reason out the diagnosis. Every topic opens in plain English, then layers in the clinical detail — so it works whether you're seeing it for the first time or reviewing for an exam.

How to use it

  • Open the Lesson Library to browse all 86 lessons grouped by body system, or use By Week to follow along with your current lecture.
  • Tap any lesson to open it. Click, drag, and toggle the controls — the picture and captions respond as you go.
  • Use your browser's Back button to return to the list. Nothing to install, no account, no ads.
  • Great on a phone or tablet for studying — and full-screen on a projector for class or study groups.

Who made it

Created by Dr. Vidya Aggrawal for nursing and health-science students, as a free way to make pathophysiology click through interaction rather than memorization.

Two lessons are full interactive calculators worth bookmarking: the ABG Interpreter and the Punnett Square Calculator — throw any values at them and they solve it step by step.

Good to know

Everything runs right in your browser and works offline, with two small exceptions that use the internet: the site-wide typeface (Google Fonts) and the one 3D Coronary Artery lesson, which streams a 3D library. Both simply look their best when you're online.

Please note: this is an independent study resource created for Nightingale College students — it is not an official Nightingale College publication, and it does not replace your course materials or your instructor's guidance. It's a free supplement, offered to help you learn.
