The waves, what each one means, and reading rhythms · Pathophysiology Week 9
An EKG is a picture of the heart's electrical signal. Each bump is one step of a heartbeat — the atria fire, a pause, the ventricles fire and squeeze, then reset. Click each wave below to see what's happening in the heart at that moment.

Reading the rhythm

Compare a normal rhythm with atrial fibrillation — the difference is spacing and P waves.
🧮 Finding the rate: count the QRS complexes (the tall spikes) in a 6-second strip and multiply by 10 = beats per minute. It's the quick bedside trick, and it still works when the rhythm is irregular like AFib.
