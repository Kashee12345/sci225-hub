Bilirubin & the Signs of Liver Disease

Follow the bile · Pathophysiology Week 14
Normally the liver packages bilirubin into bile, sends it to the gut, and it leaves as brown stool. When the liver fails or the bile can't get out, bilirubin and bile salts back up into the blood — and that one backup explains almost every sign. Click each sign to see where it comes from.

Why it happens

One idea explains it all: bilirubin/bile that should leave through the gut instead backs up into the blood → yellow skin & eyes (jaundice), itchy skin (bile salts), dark urine (kidneys dump it), and pale clay stools (none reaching the gut). Separately, a scarred liver dams the portal vein → big liver, belly fluid, and bulging esophageal veins.
