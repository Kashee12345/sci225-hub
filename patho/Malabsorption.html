Malabsorption

When the gut can't soak up nutrients · Pathophysiology Week 13
The small intestine absorbs nutrients through millions of tiny finger-like villi — they give it a huge surface area. Flatten them, remove them, or inflame them and food passes straight through: diarrhea, steatorrhea (fatty stool), and weight loss. Click each cause and watch the villi.

The intestinal lining (villi)

Nutrients (green) should get absorbed up into the villi. Whatever isn't absorbed flows on and leaves as diarrhea.

What's happening

Steatorrhea = greasy, floating, foul stool from unabsorbed fat — a hallmark of malabsorption. Celiac and short bowel both cause it; the fix is different (avoid gluten vs. maximize what bowel remains with small frequent meals, vitamins, sometimes TPN).
