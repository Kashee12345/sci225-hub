The 4 steps

Nociceptive

Normal "tissue damage" pain. Somatic (skin, muscle, bone) or visceral (organs, may refer). E.g. fracture, pancreatitis.

Neuropathic

Pain from nerve injury itself (PNS or CNS) — burning, shooting, tingling. E.g. diabetic neuropathy, shingles, carpal tunnel, Guillain-Barré.
Acute vs chronic: acute pain is protective, immediate, self-limiting, and matches the injury. Chronic pain lasts > 3–6 months, doesn't match the original injury, and brings sequelae like depression, anxiety, and fatigue.
