COPD is caused by an irritant or toxin (smoke, pollution) — not an infection — and it's permanent. It shows up two ways: chronic bronchitis (clogged, mucus-filled airways) and emphysema (destroyed, floppy alveoli). Click each to see what breaks.

What's happening

Side by side

Chronic Bronchitis

Airway problem. Too much mucus clogs the tubes → chronic productive cough, low O₂, high CO₂ (hypoxemia + hypercapnia).

Emphysema

Air-sac problem. Alveoli walls are destroyed + surfactant lost → air gets trapped, can't fully exhale → barrel chest, prolonged expiration.
Hypoxic drive: in COPD, CO₂ is chronically high, so the body starts using low O₂ as its signal to breathe. Give too much oxygen and the drive to breathe drops → respirations slow → CO₂ builds up. Titrate oxygen carefully.
