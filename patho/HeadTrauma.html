Where the blood collects · Pathophysiology Week 5
The brain is wrapped in three meninges — dura, arachnoid, pia. A head injury can bruise the brain itself or bleed into a specific layer, and where the blood collects names the bleed. Explore the bleeds, then the injury types.

What it is

Bleed cheat-sheet: Epidural = artery, above the dura, fast — think a "lucid interval" then rapid decline. Subdural = vein, below the dura, slower. Subarachnoid = thunderclap headache. Intracerebral = a hemorrhagic stroke within the tissue.
