Three Lines of Defense

How the immune system layers up · Pathophysiology Week 2
Your immune system defends in three layers: barriers that keep germs out, a fast innate response that attacks anything foreign, and a slower adaptive response that learns a specific enemy and remembers it. Click each line.

What it does

Active vs passive immunity: active = your body makes its own antibodies (from infection or a vaccine) — slow to build but long-lasting. Passive = you're given ready-made antibodies (mom's across the placenta, or an injection) — instant but temporary.
