Heart Failure

Left vs Right · Systolic vs Diastolic · Pathophysiology · Cardiovascular Part 2
Heart failure means the heart can't pump enough blood forward to meet the body's needs. Blood backs up behind the failing side — the left heart backs up into the lungs, the right heart backs up into the body. Pick a pattern below and watch where the congestion goes.

Compensation is a double-edged sword: RAAS + sympathetic drive prop up blood pressure and output short-term, but chronically they retain salt & water (more congestion), raise afterload, and drive myocardial hypertrophy — accelerating the failure.

Signs & symptoms

Which appear depends on which side is congested.

◀ Left-sided (into the lungs)

Dyspnea / SOBfluid in alveoli impairs gas exchange
Orthopnea / PNDworse lying flat / waking breathless
Crackles / ralesheard over congested lung bases
Frothy, blood-tinged sputumflash pulmonary edema
Cool, pale extremitieslow forward output

Right-sided (into the body) ▶

Peripheral edemaswollen ankles / legs
Jugular venous distentionbacked-up systemic veins
Hepatomegalycongested, enlarged liver
Ascitesfluid in the abdomen
Weight gain / fatiguefluid retention, low output
Key link: the #1 cause of right heart failure is left heart failure — chronic lung congestion raises pulmonary pressures until the right ventricle fails too. Crank left-sided severity up and watch the right side start to congest.
