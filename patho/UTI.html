Urinary Tract Infection

Lower (cystitis) vs Upper (pyelonephritis) · Pathophysiology Week 11
A UTI starts when bacteria climb up the urethra. How far they get decides how sick you are: stop at the bladder and it's a lower UTI (cystitis) — annoying but local. Climb all the way to the kidney and it's an upper UTI (pyelonephritis) — systemic and serious. Click each.

Quick differentiator

The give-away is fever & flank pain. Burning + frequency but feeling otherwise okay → cystitis (lower). Add fever, chills, flank pain, nausea → the infection reached the kidney → pyelonephritis (upper).
