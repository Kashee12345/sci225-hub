Increased Intracranial Pressure

The skull is a closed box · Pathophysiology Week 5
The skull holds three things — brain tissue, blood, and cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) — in a fixed space. Add volume (a bleed, a tumor, swelling, extra CSF) and something has to give: pressure rises, squeezing the brain. Drag the pressure up and watch the signs appear.

Cushing's triad (↓HR, ↓/irregular RR, ↑BP with a widening pulse pressure) is a LATE, ominous sign of dangerously high ICP — the brainstem is being compressed. Early signs are just headache, nausea, vomiting. Uncontrolled, the brain herniates (e.g. uncal herniation compresses the brainstem, which controls breathing and heart rate).
