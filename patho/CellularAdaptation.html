Cellular Adaptation

How cells cope with stress · Pathophysiology Week 3
When cells are stressed, they adapt to survive — by getting bigger, smaller, more numerous, or by changing type. Some adaptations are harmless; one (dysplasia) is a warning sign for cancer. Click each and watch the cells change.

What's happening

When adaptation fails → cell injury. The #1 cause of cell injury is hypoxia (from atherosclerosis, anemia, or ischemic stroke). Mild injury is reversible; if the stress continues, it becomes irreversible and the cell dies.
