Hemostasis: the platelet plug + the fibrin mesh · Pathophysiology Week 9
Stopping a bleed takes two steps: first platelets pile up to form a quick plug (needs platelets & von Willebrand factor), then clotting factors weave a fibrin mesh to lock it in place. Too little of either → bleeding. Too much → clots. Click a disorder to see which step breaks.

Thinning the blood — the two anticoagulants

💧 Heparin
Works on the fibrin-mesh step — boosts antithrombin to shut down thrombin & factor Xa. Fast, given IV/injection, monitored with aPTT. (Note: heparin can itself cause thrombocytopenia — HIT.)
💊 Warfarin
Blocks the liver from making the vitamin-K clotting factors (II, VII, IX, X). Slow to work, taken by mouth, monitored with PT/INR.
