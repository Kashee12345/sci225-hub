Edema — Two Pressures

Why fluid leaks into the tissues · Pathophysiology Week 1
At every capillary, two forces fight over the fluid: hydrostatic pressure pushes water out of the vessel, and oncotic pressure (created by albumin) pulls it back in. Tip that balance — too much push or too little pull — and fluid floods the tissue as edema.

What's happening

↑ Hydrostatic (push)

Too much pressure inside pushes fluid out. Causes: heart failure, venous congestion, fluid overload.

↓ Oncotic (pull)

Not enough albumin to pull fluid back. Causes: liver failure, malnutrition, nephrotic syndrome.
Two ways to flood the tissue: turn the push up (hydrostatic) or the pull down (oncotic). Either way, net fluid moves into the interstitium → edema. Albumin is the key protein holding water in the vessel.
