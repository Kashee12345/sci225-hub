GERD & the Esophagus

Acid where it shouldn't be · Pathophysiology Week 13
A ring of muscle — the lower esophageal sphincter (LES) — is supposed to snap shut and keep stomach acid down where it belongs. When it stays loose, acid refluxes up into the esophagus (GERD). Do that for years and the lining changes, step by step, all the way to cancer. Explore both.

What's happening

The GERD staircase: acid → esophagitis (inflamed) → stricture (scar narrows it, trouble swallowing) and ulcers (bright-red bleeding) → Barrett's (the lining turns pre-cancerous) → esophageal cancer. Risk stackers: chronic GERD, alcohol, and smoking.
