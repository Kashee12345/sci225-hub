When the chromosome count is off · Pathophysiology Week 7
A nondisjunction error in meiosis leaves an egg or sperm with an extra chromosome (trisomy) or a missing one (monosomy). A karyotype — a photo of all the chromosomes — reveals it. Click each classic disorder.

The karyotype clue

The relevant chromosome pair — normal is two; these disorders have three or one.

What it is

Count it out: Trisomy = an extra chromosome (three instead of two) — Down (T21) and Klinefelter (XXY). Monosomy = a missing one — Turner (a single X, "XO").
