Sliding Filament Contraction

Muscles don't shorten their filaments — they slide them past each other. Myosin heads grab actin and ratchet it inward, pulling the Z-lines together. The filaments stay the same length; the sarcomere gets shorter. Watch it.

💡 During contraction the A-band stays the same width (it's the length of myosin), but the I-band and H-zone shrink as actin slides in. That's the giveaway on an exam.
