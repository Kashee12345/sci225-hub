Oxygen Transport

A red blood cell is a delivery truck for oxygen. Watch it load O₂ in the lungs (turning bright red) and drop it off in the tissues (turning darker), on a loop.

The oxygen loop

Oxygen transportlungs ↔ tissues

How it works

Hemoglobin is 4 protein chains, each with an iron-containing heme — so one RBC's hemoglobin carries up to 4 O₂ each, and a single red cell holds ~1 billion oxygen molecules.
